FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Yes, as long as you are an experienced shooter. Learning the basics in the dark is not safe or fun. Read the First-Timer Guide for a complete walkthrough.
Four divisions across two nights. Friday: NV 2Gun and NV PCC — night vision required, white light prohibited. Saturday: 2Gun (rifle + pistol) and PCC (pistol caliber carbine, optional pistol). See the Events page for full details.
Night vision is required only for Friday NV divisions. Saturday 2Gun and PCC are run in complete darkness using competitor-supplied white light — headlamps, weapon lights, and flashlights are all permitted.
You are responsible for all illumination. The course is well-marked but completely dark. Bring a weapon-mounted light, a headlamp, and a backup flashlight at minimum. Fresh batteries and backups are essential — a light failure on course costs time. NV competitors: white light is not permitted during Friday divisions.
Your final score is your combined total time: the sum of all four shooting stage times plus your total course run time. Lower time wins. Missed targets add 20–60 seconds per miss depending on the stage. 50% run, 50% shoot.
2Gun (Saturday): center-fire rifle 5.45×39 or larger + center-fire pistol 9×19mm or larger. PCC (Saturday): pistol caliber carbine. NV divisions follow the same firearm requirements as their Saturday counterparts. All firearms semi-auto only.
