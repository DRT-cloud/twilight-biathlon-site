NIGHT VISION DIVISION

Friday night. NV only. White light prohibited. Compete in the dark.

The Twilight Biathlon dedicates an entire night — Friday —to night vision competitors. This event structurally isolates NV divisions from white-light divisions on a separate night, eliminating scoring ambiguity and creating two distinct event experiences.

NV Divisions

Friday — Night Vision Only
NV 2GUN
  • Night Vision Only · White Light Prohibited
  • Rifle: center-fire, 5.45×39 or larger
  • Pistol: 9×19mm or larger
  • NV device required
  • Rifle: 30–50 rds · Pistol: 40–60 rds
  • $100 entry ($50 second division)
Friday — Night Vision Only
NV PCC
  • Night Vision Only · White Light Prohibited
  • PCC: pistol caliber carbine (primary)
  • Pistol optional
  • NV device required
  • PCC: 70–110 rds
  • $100 entry ($50 second division)

What Makes This Different

Most events that offer NV divisions run them on the same night as white-light divisions. Competitors share the course, and the experience is compromised — white-light runners illuminate the path NV competitors are navigating in the dark, and scoring can be ambiguous when different lighting types share stages.

At the Twilight Biathlon:

  • Friday is NV-only. White light is limited on the course. ROs use filtered or very dim lights at stages.
  • White light is prohibited for NV competitors except for admin, safety reasons, medical emergencies, or when directed by an RO. No visible light for stages or course navigation.
  • First offense: verbal warning. Second offense: moved to the corresponding Saturday white-light division with scores transferred.

NV Gear Requirements

  • Night vision device: any functional NVD. Monoculars (PVS-14), binoculars (DTNVS, BNVD), thermal clip-ons, or digital NV are all permitted.
  • IR illuminators: permitted on rifle, pistol, helmet, or handheld.
  • IR lasers: permitted. IR only aiming devices allowed.
  • Helmet mount recommended but not required.

Entry-level path: A PVS-14 monocular with a basic helmet/skull-crusher mount and an IR illuminator on your rifle is the minimum recommended NV setup. You do not need dual-tube NV or thermal to compete. Many competitors run single-tube successfully.

Tips for NV Division

  • Practice navigating under NV. Running uneven terrain under NV is different from static shooting under NV. Depth perception changes. Practice before the event.
  • Zero your IR laser. Your primary aiming method. Confirm zero at the distance you expect to engage targets at 5–150 yards.
  • Bring backup batteries for your NVD. A dead NV device on course means you're running blind.
  • Chem-lights. Useful for marking yourself in the stage waiting area without generating much light.

Run Both Nights

You can compete in a Friday NV division and a Saturday white-light division. Second division entry is $50. This is the best way to experience both formats in a single event weekend.
