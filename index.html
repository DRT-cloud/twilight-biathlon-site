NIGHTTIME RUN N' GUN

The Twilight Biathlon is a timed 5K run-and-shoot competition held at night on unlit terrain at The Burial Mound Shooting Center in Pawnee, Oklahoma. Competitors run a two-lap course through Oklahoma fields and woods, engaging four live-fire shooting stages along the route. Four divisions cover the field: 2Gun and PCC run Saturday night on white light, while NV 2Gun and NV PCC run Friday night on night vision only with no white light permitted. Scoring is time-based — your final score is the combined total of your four shooting stage times plus your course run time, with the lowest score winning.

1
The Original

The original nighttime run-and-gun biathlon in the United States — running continuously since 2018.

2
Dedicated NV Divisions

Dedicated Night Vision divisions on Friday — NV 2Gun and NV PCC with full white-light prohibition. A format almost no other event offers.

3
Timed Release

Individual timed releases every 5 minutes. Every competitor gets a clean, uncontested course with shorter wait times.

4
Engineered Against Bottlenecks

Two-lap odd/even stage rotation. Even competitors run even stages on lap one, odd on lap two.

5
Genuine Unlit Terrain

Oklahoma fields, woods, and creek beds. Tests navigation and light discipline, not just shooting fitness.

Rules & Procedures

Section 1 — Safety Rules

1.1 — Competitor Responsibility
It is the competitor's responsibility to read and understand the rules and stage briefings and to agree to be bound by them while participating in the event.

1.2 — Basic Firearms Safety Rules

  1. Treat all guns as if they are always loaded.
  2. Never let the muzzle cover anything you are not willing to destroy.
  3. Keep your finger off the trigger until your sights are on target and you have made a positive identification.
  4. Be sure of your target, what is in front of it, and what is behind it.

1.3 — Cold Range
All events operate on a COLD RANGE. Firearms shall be unloaded at all times except when directly under the supervision of a Range Officer (RO) during an active Course of Fire (COF).

  1. Rifles and PCCs shall be transported between stages with detachable magazines removed and chamber empty. Slings strongly recommended.
  2. Pistols shall remain holstered at all times between stages. Chamber shall be empty; a magazine may be inserted.
  3. Competitors shall not handle, manipulate, or load any firearm outside of the active shooting stage under RO supervision. Any such action observed between stages is grounds for Match DQ under Section 9.

1.4 — Eye and Ear Protection
Eye and ear protection must be worn at all times while at a shooting stage. Ear protection is mandatory from "Make Ready" through "Range is Clear." Electronic hearing protection is strongly recommended.

1.5 — Minimum Target Illumination Standard

  1. A competitor shall not engage any target unless positively identified using lighting or night vision before the trigger is pressed. Firing at sound, movement, or assumed target location is prohibited — immediate Match DQ.
  2. ROs shall visually confirm the shooting lane is clear before issuing "Make Ready."

1.6 — Abandoning Firearms

  1. During a COF, a competitor may be required to abandon a firearm to transition to another. No more than one gun in hands at any time.
  2. Designated firearm abandonment locations will be identified during the stage brief, marked with high-visibility markers.
  3. Firearms shall be abandoned with: MANUAL SAFETY ENGAGED, or EMPTY.
  4. Abandoned firearms shall be placed muzzle-forward into the designated receptacle or laid flat in the designated zone, pointed downrange.

Section 2 — Range Commands and Procedures

  1. Make Ready: RO supervises competitor through firearm preparation.
  2. Are You Ready / Standby: issued at start position before timer activation. Silence indicates readiness. State "Not Ready" if not ready.
  3. STOP: any RO may call STOP at any time. Immediately cease firing and stop moving.
  4. Unload and Show Clear: lower firearm, present muzzle downrange, magazine removed, chamber empty for RO inspection.
  5. Range is Clear: issued after all firearms confirmed clear. No downrange movement until this call.
  6. Stage Brief Supersedes Posted Information.
  7. Competitor Self-Sufficiency: carry all required equipment for the full course from the start. No resupply at vehicles.
  8. Stage Wait Time and Stopwatch: when arriving at a stage during an active COF, proceed to Stage Waiting Area (SWA) at least 5 yards behind the shooting line. Start personal stopwatch immediately. Turn off all lights except chem-lights. Remain until invited by RO. RO records wait time — deducted from run time.
  9. Equipment Carry: carry all personal equipment throughout each stage unless stage brief states otherwise.

Section 3 — Firearms

  1. Serviceability: all firearms must be safe and serviceable. ROs may inspect at any time.
  2. Malfunction Repair: may be attempted under RO supervision, without outside assistance, on run time.
  3. Fire Mode: semi-automatic only. No full-auto or select-fire.
  4. Slings: required on all rifles and PCCs. Firearm must remain attached to competitor during transitions. Unslung rifles carried in-hand between stages are prohibited.

Section 4 — Holsters and Equipment

4.1 — Handgun Holsters:

  1. Must safely retain the handgun during vigorous movement including running on uneven terrain.
  2. Material must be rigid and completely cover the trigger (semi-autos) or cylinder (revolvers).
  3. Prohibited: shoulder holsters, belly bands, magazine pouch holsters, cross-draw chest holsters, pocket holsters.

Section 5 — Ammunition

  1. Prohibited: tracer, incendiary, armor-piercing, steel/tungsten core. Steel-cased with non-steel projectiles is permitted.
  2. Pistol/PCC: 9×19mm or larger. FN 5.7 not permitted.
  3. Rifle: 5.45×39mm or larger. MD has discretion on uncommon calibers.
  4. Single projectile only. No shot shells or multi-projectile loads.

Section 6 — Targets

  1. Stage brief specifies target type, count, required firearm, and hit count.
  2. Ask questions before "Make Ready." Wrong firearm = procedural penalty or Stage DQ.
  3. Night Target Standards:
    1. High-visibility contrast: white targets on dark backstops or black targets on white backers.
    2. Target faces not illuminated by stage lighting — competitors illuminate using personal lighting.
    3. Rifle/PCC: 5–150 yards. Pistol: 5–50 yards.

Section 7 — Scoring

  1. Disputes appealed to stage RO. MD/RM reviews if unresolved. Run time continues during appeals.
  2. Shoot/run weighting: 50/50.
  3. Targets must be neutralized to avoid time penalties.
  4. Neutralization standards:
    1. Paper/cardboard: required hits in scored zone.
    2. Steel knockdown plates: must fall to score.
    3. Static plates: struck required number of times.
    4. ROs call "HIT" audibly for non-obvious hits. At night, secondary visual signal (shoulder tap) used when hearing protection may mask calls.

Section 8 — Stage Penalties

All stage penalties are fixed time additions. Standard penalty: 20–60 seconds per occurrence (varies by stage):

  1. Failure to Neutralize (FTN): one hit when multiple required.
  2. No-Shoot: hit on designated friendly or no-shoot target.
  3. Procedural violation: failure to follow stage procedures, wrong position, shooting under walls, failing to engage targets in required order.

Section 9 — Match Disqualifications

Match DQ causes (immediate removal from event):

  1. Cheating
  2. Intentionally cutting the course
  3. Altering course, COF, or scorecards
  4. Unsportsmanlike conduct
  5. Discharge aimed over a berm or significantly off-target in unsafe direction
  6. Round chambered in any firearm while not under direct RO supervision
  7. Competitor impaired by drugs or alcohol
  8. Firing without positive visual identification — sound, movement, or assumed position in the dark is an immediate Match DQ
  9. Deliberate use of lighting to blind or damage night-vision equipment of another competitor

Section 10 — Stage Disqualifications

Stage DQ causes (no score for stage):

  1. Negligent discharge not in direction of a target (includes during loading, reloading, malfunction remediation, or movement)
  2. Dropping or losing control of a loaded firearm after "Make Ready"
  3. Breaking the 180-degree safety plane
  4. Muzzle sweeping any part of competitor's own body during COF
  5. Engaging pistol-designated targets with a rifle caliber
  6. Firing after "STOP" command, after stage complete, or before timer

Section 11 — Appeals

Decisions made by stage RO. RM/MD final ruling if disputed. Run time continues during appeals. Safety violations not subject to arbitration.

Section 12 — Lighting

12.1 — Standard Divisions (2Gun and PCC, Saturday):

  1. Weapon-mounted lights, illuminators, and non-IR lasers permitted.
  2. Any personal lighting permitted at stages and during navigation.
  3. Weapon-mounted lights shall not be used as primary navigation tool between stages. Firearm must be slung/holstered during navigation. A separate headlamp or handheld light is required for course navigation.

12.2 — Stage Lighting Discipline (All Divisions):

  1. When arriving at a stage during active COF, turn off all personal lights except chem-lights upon entering the SWA.
  2. Only stage lighting, RO lights, and active shooter's lights permitted during an active COF.
  3. Violation: 30-second stage time penalty per occurrence.

12.3 — NV Division Lighting Rules (Friday Only):

  1. NV competitors may use NVDs, IR illuminators, and IR lasers.
  2. White light prohibited for NV competitors except: designated Safety Areas, medical emergencies, or when directed by RO.
  3. Non-NV staff use red-filtered lights at NV stages.
  4. First offense: verbal warning. Second offense: moved to corresponding white-light division (NV 2Gun → 2Gun, NV PCC → PCC). Scores transferred.

Section 13 — Medical and Emergency Procedures

  1. Any person on course may call "CEASE FIRE" for a medical emergency. All shooting halts immediately. ROs acknowledge before resuming.
  2. Competitors not finishing within 2 hours flagged by scoring station. RO or MD initiates contact.
  3. MD has sole authority to suspend or abort the event for severe weather, lightning, or course hazard.

Section 14 — Refund Policy

  1. Full refund minus fees until 60 days before event.
  2. Half refund minus fees until 30 days before event.
  3. No refund within 30 days of event.
