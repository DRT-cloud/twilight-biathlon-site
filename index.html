The Twilight Biathlon is a timed 5K run-and-shoot competition held at night on unlit terrain at The Burial Mound Shooting Center in Pawnee, Oklahoma. Competitors run a two-lap course through Oklahoma fields and woods, engaging four live-fire shooting stages along the route. Four divisions cover the field: 2Gun and PCC run Saturday night on white light, while NV 2Gun and NV PCC run Friday night on night vision only with no white light permitted. Scoring is time-based — your final score is the combined total of your four shooting stage times plus your course run time, with the lowest score winning.

1
The Original

The original nighttime run n gun biathlon — running continuously since 2018.

2
Dedicated NV Divisions

Dedicated Night Vision divisions on Friday — NV 2Gun and NV PCC with full white-light prohibition. A format almost no other event offers.

3
Timed Release

Individual timed releases every 5 minutes. Every competitor gets a clean, uncontested course with shorter wait times.

4
Engineered Against Bottlenecks

Two-lap odd/even stage rotation. Even competitors run even stages on lap one, odd on lap two.

5
Genuine Unlit Terrain

Oklahoma fields, woods, and creek beds. Tests navigation and light discipline, not just shooting fitness.
